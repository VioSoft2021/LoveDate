★ FINAL DIRECTION — Pure wordmark restraint

Saint Laurent philosophy

No separate icon. No auxiliary symbol. No monogram. The wordmark IS the entire brand identity — used at every scale, from billboard to favicon. The app icon is a cropped fragment of the wordmark itself ("P"), kept in the same Bodoni Moda typeface so it reads as part of the same system. This is the philosophy Saint Laurent, Balenciaga, The Row, and Maison Margiela use at their most confident.

Why this works

1. The wordmark carries everything. No "icon plus wordmark" split — one system. Bodoni Moda is the typeface of Vogue, Bulgari, Italian Vogue covers since the 1960s. Wearing Bodoni Moda Black = wearing Italian luxury vocabulary.

2. The elongated É accent is the only embellishment. Above the entire word. It's your equivalent of FedEx's hidden arrow — quietly present in every rendering.

3. The app icon is a fragment of the wordmark. Just "P" in the same Bodoni Moda — so the icon and wordmark are visibly the same family. No need for a separate symbolic mark to "represent" the brand.

4. It ages 30 years. Saint Laurent's logo from 1961 still works. Bodoni from 1798 still works. This won't look "of 2026" — it'll look timeless.

Direction E (NEW) — Artistic P monogram with brand-signature accent

Iteration 3 · heart removed

Pure monogram. The P is rendered in Cinzel serif (same family as the wordmark) at large weight. Above it sits the horizontal accent line — the SAME line that crowns the É in the wordmark. That shared element ties the icon and wordmark into one brand system. The icon "wears the same crown" as the É. Hermès-tier confidence; no gimmicks.
